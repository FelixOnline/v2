Sexism call for evidence launched at Imperial

Becky Lane, Deputy President (Welfare) launches a call for everyone, lecturers and students, to share their experiences of sexism

By Rebecca M Lane
Friday, 9th November 2012

Let me start by introducing myself, I am Becky Lane and I am this year’s Deputy President (Welfare) and I have just finished my Physics degree at Imperial College. Although my experiences here as an undergraduate here at Imperial have generally been fine (aside from the OH-MY-GOD-EXAMS-ARE-SO-BAD) there are times that I have felt marginalised because of my gender. In my little bubble of friends I remained on the whole oblivious, until I stepped into the spotlight cast by Union politics and elections. I was generally an outsider to the Union fold before I ran for my Sabbatical position. I won’t dredge up the past, but during campaigning I was subjected to derogatory comments based upon my gender. I was incredibly angry, taking the point of view that I am happy for people to criticise me based upon my views, but not my looks.

This year, the Physics society has already been mentioned in the Independent regarding an email sent out which caused offence to some female first year students and rightly so. The attitudes towards this email that I have come across have been at times quite frankly horrifying. Such as ‘the person who sent this email was a female so therefore it can’t be sexist’.
